
# Servitized Business Models

| Type of Servitized Business Model | Short Definition | Example |
|---------------------------------|------------------|---------|
| Product-oriented service provider | Sells a core product with additional services that support product use and enhance customer value. | A manufacturer of medical imaging equipment selling MRI machines with optional maintenance contracts and training services. |
| Industrializer | Provides standardized, modular, and repeatable services at scale, focusing on efficiency and cost reduction. | A cloud infrastructure provider offering automated and scalable IT maintenance and monitoring services. |
| Customized integrated solution provider | Delivers tailored, end-to-end solutions integrating products, services, and software to solve specific customer problems. | An industrial automation company delivering a customized smart factory solution. |
| Outcome provider | Charges customers based on achieved outcomes or performance rather than on products or services delivered. | An aircraft engine manufacturer charging airlines based on engine uptime or flight hours (“power by the hour”). |
| Platform provider | Offers a platform that enables interactions among multiple actors in an ecosystem, creating value through coordination and network effects. | A digital health platform connecting patients, healthcare providers, developers, and insurers. |
